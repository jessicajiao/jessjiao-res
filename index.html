As a young child, I was heavily exposed to the world of writing and education. It greatly intrigued me and as such, I of course simply just had to decide that I wanted to choose to go to university in order to pursue said career in english and further my childhood aspirations. Of course, this only further increased my passion as I went through the rigorous classes here and I have enjoyed all the unique experiences this school has offered.

I became a translator for my parents at a young age. I picked up Mandarin and Cantonese before ever stepping foot in a school, and the English language opened many doors, enabling me to help my parents understand. My parents, as Chinese immigrants, did not know a modicum of English but saw America as an opportunity for my future and worked hard to provide financial support. I struggled with the responsibilities of answering phone calls, interpreting mail, buying essentials, and arranging medical appointments. Constantly switching between languages and grasping key concepts such as taxes as a child was stressful: I realized the freedom of being able to convey my words properly. Communication is a broad field.
